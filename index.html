Research

My research interests include...

  • Codon Usage Bias: Codon usage bias (CUB) refers to the preference for certain codons over others in the encoding of amino acids in proteins. This bias is influenced by various factors such as mutational pressures, natural selection, gene expression levels, and tRNA abundance. Understanding CUB provides insights into gene expression efficiency, evolutionary constraints, and adaptation mechanisms at the molecular level.
  • Genomic Evolution: Genomic evolution encompasses the changes in genetic material over time within and between species. These changes are driven by processes such as mutation, natural selection, genetic drift, and recombination. Studying genomic evolution helps uncover patterns of diversification, adaptation, and the molecular mechanisms that underlie evolutionary change across genomes.
  • Phylogenetic Reconstruction: Phylogenetic analysis involves reconstructing evolutionary relationships between organisms or genes by comparing their genetic sequences. It allows scientists to infer common ancestry, evolutionary divergence, and the evolutionary history of species or gene families. This method is essential in understanding how species or genes have evolved over time and can reveal insights into functional adaptations.
